Design Form

Initial Renderings

Early 3D renderings drew inspiration from the Nintendo Wii remote and nunchuck, providing a familiar and ergonomic reference point. The intent was to reduce the learning curve by using forms already understood by gamers.

User Testing

During development, our team initially focused on a single ambidextrous concept. While inclusive, this approach constrained form factor flexibility. To address this, user testing and feedback were incorporated to refine the physical design around comfort, reachability, and hand fit. User testing was conducted in two rounds to evaluate the ergonomics, usability, and comfort of the SPUD controller design. Each round provided valuable insights to refine the product form and user interaction.

Round 1

The initial round of user testing was structured to guide the controller's design direction. Participants were provided with a selection of buttons and modeling clay, and invited to freely prototype their vision of a one-handed controller tailored for first-person shooter (FPS) games.

Round 2

After the form was in place we were tasked with finding the preferred feeling for all of the buttons. This was accomplished by using controllers from trhoughout the genreations to get a sense of what feelings people liked, weither that was more tactile or a softer button press

Design Iterations

Our design process evolved through multiple iterations, each bringing us closer to the optimal solution for one-handed gaming accessibility.

Our initial design focused on ergonomic comfort and essential gaming functions. This prototype established the core concept of one-handed FPS gaming, but lacked a lot of functionality.

The second iteration evolved into an ambidextrous design, accommodating both left and right-handed users. This version introduced additional buttons and refined motion controls, while maintaining the compact form factor.

Second Digital Design

The second digital model included a lot more functionality as well as a more ergonomic design. Straying away from the ambidextrous design, we focused on creating a more ergonomic design that was more comfortable for one-handed use. It also included a wrist strap that we heard a lot of people wanted in the first round of testing.

Third Design

The third iteration marked a significant evolution in our design process, introducing a more streamlined form factor. This version emphasized improved ergonomics with a curved grip design and optimized button placement based on user testing feedback. The model features integrated support structures and refined motion control housing, representing a major step toward our final design. This was also the point at which we designed to include buttons on the side of the controller spanning the entire controller.

Design Evolution

This visual progression showcases SPUD's complete design evolution, from initial concept to final form. Each iteration brought significant improvements in ergonomics, functionality, and user experience. The journey reflects our commitment to user-centered design, with each version incorporating feedback and technological refinements to create an optimal one-handed gaming controller.

Final Design

Our final design iteration represents the culmination of our development process, incorporating all the lessons learned from previous versions. This model features enhanced ergonomics, refined button positioning, and improved internal component organization. The design prioritizes both functionality and user comfort, with special attention paid to the grip angle and trigger placement for optimal gaming performance.
